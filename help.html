Yahtzee Tutorial

To play yahtzee on this website you need pen and paper to save your scores. Ideally you get a yahtzee sheet online.
Bear in mind that you are not supposed to play same scoring twice. For example you cannot score chance twice when you're playing.
So if you were to get two ones, score two points and then the next turn you get a single one, you cannot score the single point because you've already used up that slot. Though you can score ones on a new slot if you were to get them as three or four pairs as three of a kind or four of a kind, but here aswell, only once.

The primary rules you need to remember for this program is how straights, yahtzee and full house work:

Full house: Three of a kind and two of another kind. Gives 25 points.
Small Straight: Ladder of four numbers. Ex. 1-2-3-4 or 2-3-4-5 or 3-4-5-6. Gives 30 points
Large Straight: Ladder of four numbers. Ex. 1-2-3-4-5 or 2-3-4-5-6. Gives 40 points
Yahtzee: Five of a kind gives 50 points automatically!
Also bear in mind that this program is a work in progress, therefore we haven't developed a way
for you to skip the turn when you are content with you score. If you are certain of your score just lock
all dices in and roll out until you get the reset button. In time an extra reset button without conditions will be added.

When it comes to pairs and chance, the most common score you'll get on a game of yahtzee,
the game shows you those scores so you don't need to calculate anything while playing.
